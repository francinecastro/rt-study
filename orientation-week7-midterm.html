Study Guide

Chapter 1 — Cancer Overview

  • Cell cycle: G0, G1, S, G2, M.
  • Most radiosensitive: G2 and M phases.
  • Benign tumors: slow, encapsulated, pushing, no metastasis, usually well differentiated.
  • Malignant tumors: invasive, infiltrating, metastasize, abnormal cells, may be poorly differentiated or anaplastic.
  • Anaplastic: cells are so changed they do not look like original tissue.
  • Sarcoma: mesenchymal/connective tissue origin: bone, cartilage, muscle, fat.
  • Carcinoma: epithelial tissue origin: covers surfaces and lines organs/cavities.
  • Etiology: cause of disease. Epidemiology: incidence of disease.
  • Screening: must be sensitive and specific.
  • TNM: T = tumor, N = nodes, M = metastasis.
  • Grade: aggressiveness based on differentiation.
  • Biopsy: only way to know for certain if a lump/growth is cancerous.
  • Neoadjuvant: before surgery. Adjuvant: with/after surgery or chemo.
  • Brachytherapy: short-distance radiation using radioactive sources.

Chapter 2 — Ethics

  • ASRT: professional society for imaging and radiation therapy.
  • ARRT: credentialing body for certification and registration.
  • Ethics: moral duty, values, right and wrong.
  • 7 principles: autonomy, beneficence, confidentiality, justice, nonmaleficence, role fidelity, veracity.
  • HIPAA: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act.
  • Teleology: focuses on consequences.
  • Deontology: focuses on rules/right and wrong.
  • Virtue ethics: practical wisdom and character.
  • Grief cycle: shock, denial, anger, bargaining, depression, testing, acceptance.
  • Assault: threat. Battery: touching without consent. False imprisonment: preventing patient from leaving.
  • Libel: written defamation. Slander: spoken defamation.

Chapter 3 — Patient Assessment

  • Therapeutic relationship: healthcare worker and patient working together for beneficial change.
  • Cognitive content: facts and words.
  • Affective content: feelings, attitudes, behaviors.
  • 10 verbal responses: minimal verbal response, reflecting, paraphrasing, probing, clarifying, interpreting, checking out, informing, confronting, summarizing.
  • Empathy: identifying with another person’s feelings, thoughts, or experiences.
  • Cachexia: loss of muscle with or without loss of fat.
  • Myelosuppression: reduced bone marrow function.
  • Anemia: low RBCs. Leukopenia: low WBCs. Thrombocytopenia: low platelets. Pancytopenia: reduction in all blood cells.
  • Orthostatic hypotension: sudden BP drop when standing.
  • Transfer rule: transfer toward the patient’s stronger side.
